# KEY North Star

## The Simple Mission

> **KEY creates worlds where the story never stops being the game.**

KEY is a modular cognitive world engine for creating living, playable realities. It is designed to understand what is happening, remember what has happened, preserve a coherent truth, and turn meaningful choices into an evolving story.

This document is a plain-language alignment guide. It does not replace the Constitution, Manifesto, Lexicon, formal architecture, or research program. It connects those foundations to the experience we are trying to create and the work directly in front of us.

## Why KEY Exists

Many games begin with a compelling story and gradually settle into a familiar loop:

```text
story
↓
tutorial
↓
grind
↓
loot
↓
repeat
```

The systems continue operating, but the story becomes secondary.

KEY begins from a different premise:

```text
situation
↓
choice
↓
consequence
↓
new situation
↓
choice
↓
consequence
```

The narrative does not pause while gameplay happens.

**Gameplay is the story.**

## What the Player Should Experience

A player should be able to enter a KEY world, attempt something naturally, and feel that the world genuinely understood and responded.

The experience should follow a simple cycle:

```text
Player acts
↓
The world understands the intent
↓
Reality determines what is possible
↓
The world changes
↓
People remember and respond
↓
The story continues
```

The player is not merely choosing among visible branches written in advance. The player is participating in a reality with its own truth, history, people, pressures, and open future.

## What Makes a World Feel Alive

A world is not alive because it generates more dialogue or contains more content.

It feels alive because:

- time matters;
- people have limited knowledge and personal beliefs;
- characters pursue goals beyond the player's attention;
- relationships change and retain history;
- actions and inaction create consequences;
- resources and physical constraints are real;
- discoveries reveal existing truth rather than inventing convenient answers;
- the world continues to produce new situations through its own operation.

Every system should contribute to meaningful possibility. Weather matters when it changes what people can do. Resources matter when scarcity forces choices. Politics matters when power changes lives. Memory matters when the past returns through trust, fear, loyalty, resentment, opportunity, or loss.

## The Role of Artificial Intelligence

AI is not the final authority over reality.

AI may help interpret, propose, imagine, describe, and surprise. The world engine decides what becomes true.

In plain language:

> **AI proposes. The world decides.**

This protects the world from contradiction and allows language models, parsers, narrators, interfaces, and other tools to improve without replacing the underlying reality.

## Stable Center, Replaceable Edges

KEY should remain flexible without becoming fragile.

The stable center preserves:

- canonical world truth;
- time and causality;
- validated change;
- character knowledge boundaries;
- persistent consequence;
- auditable history.

The replaceable edges may include:

- language interpreters;
- AI models;
- narrators;
- interfaces;
- presentation layers;
- specialized simulation modules.

Modularity is not a goal by itself. It exists so KEY can evolve without repeatedly rebuilding its foundation or becoming permanently dependent on one technology.

## Why Winter Medicine Exists

Winter Medicine is not the final product and it is not a miniature version of every future KEY world.

It is a laboratory.

Its purpose is to test the smallest behaviors that must work before the project expands:

- Can a player express an intention naturally?
- Can the system distinguish interpretation from reality?
- Can an action be checked against the actual world?
- Can time advance coherently?
- Can state changes remain causal and auditable?
- Can characters know different things?
- Can consequences become the next part of the story?

A small world makes mistakes visible. It allows KEY to prove its foundation before scale hides weaknesses beneath generated content.

## Where We Are Now

The project has already established a substantial philosophical, research, and architectural foundation.

The active engineering challenge is to turn those principles into a reliable playable loop:

```text
natural player input
↓
interpreted intent
↓
validated action proposal
↓
canonical state change
↓
character and world response
↓
continued narrative
```

The immediate goal is not maximum intelligence or unlimited player freedom. It is a trustworthy foundation that can support increasingly capable interpreters and increasingly complex worlds without surrendering coherence.

## The North Star Test

Every major feature or architectural decision should be tested with three questions.

### Player Test

Does this make the player feel more like they are participating in a living world?

### Story Test

Does this create meaningful choices, consequences, memories, relationships, discoveries, or future possibilities?

### Architecture Test

Does this strengthen the foundation without creating unnecessary complexity or locking KEY to a temporary technology?

A feature that fails these tests should be simplified, reconsidered, or removed.

## What KEY Is Not

KEY is not:

- an endless text generator;
- a chatbot pretending to be a world;
- a collection of disconnected AI prompts;
- a predetermined story disguised as freedom;
- a grind loop with generated dialogue layered on top;
- a system where anything happens merely because the player requested it;
- a project whose vision changes whenever a new model or technology appears.

## Long-Term Direction

KEY may eventually support fantasy kingdoms, science-fiction colonies, mysteries, political dramas, historical simulations, educational worlds, novels, film and television development, and forms of interactive storytelling that do not yet have names.

A future creator might describe a world in ordinary language:

> Create a world ruled by dragons, where humanity survives inside competing mountain kingdoms and the gods have stopped answering prayers.

KEY's task would not simply be to generate lore. It would create a coherent reality capable of remembering, changing, surprising, and producing meaningful stories through interaction.

That larger vision remains important, but the path toward it is disciplined:

> **Build the smallest honest proof, learn from it, and expand only when the foundation can carry more weight.**

## The Promise

KEY does not promise that anything can happen.

It promises that what happens will matter.

KEY does not write the story instead of the player.

It creates a world capable of becoming their story.

> **We are building worlds that understand, remember, and evolve — worlds where the story never stops being the game.**
