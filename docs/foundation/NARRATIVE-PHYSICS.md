# KEY Narrative Physics

## Status

Version 0.1 — provisional laws under active study.

Narrative Physics describes recurring relationships among state, agency, pressure, knowledge, consequence, and possibility. These are not universal rules of art. They are falsifiable hypotheses intended to guide research and architecture.

## Evidence Standard

Every proposed law must record:

- statement
- scope
- supporting evidence
- weakening evidence
- confidence
- related hypotheses
- engineering implications
- next test

No law is promoted because it sounds compatible with the Manifesto.

## NP-001 — Conservation of Consequence

### Statement

Important state change remains active until it is transformed, resolved, forgotten through a credible process, or overridden by a stronger change.

### Current Evidence

- CASE-0001 — *The Matrix*
- CASE-0003 — *Mad Max: Fury Road*
- CASE-0004 — *Children of Men*

### Weakening Evidence

Compressed, episodic, comic, surreal, or intentionally discontinuous forms may reduce persistent consequence without producing failure.

### Confidence

Moderate.

### Related Hypotheses

H-001, H-005, H-008, H-014.

### Engineering Implication

The Memory Engine must retain causal links between events and persistent state.

## NP-002 — Situated Knowledge

### Statement

Credible action follows from what an agent can perceive, remember, infer, believe, and imagine rather than from objective truth unavailable to that agent.

### Current Evidence

- CASE-0001
- CASE-0003
- CASE-0004

### Confidence

Moderate.

### Related Hypotheses

H-003, H-007, H-013.

### Engineering Implication

Canonical world state and agent knowledge state must remain separate.

## NP-003 — Relationship Memory

### Statement

Relationships accumulate history, and later choices gain or lose emotional credibility according to whether that history remains active.

### Current Evidence

- Neo, Morpheus, Trinity, and Cypher in CASE-0001
- Furiosa, Max, Nux, and the Wives in CASE-0003
- Theo, Julian, Kee, and Jasper in CASE-0004

### Confidence

Moderate.

### Related Hypotheses

H-002, H-004, H-008.

### Engineering Implication

Relationship state must be directional, historical, and event-linked rather than a single affinity score.

## NP-004 — Pressure Through Constraint

### Statement

Narrative pressure rises when remaining alternatives become more costly, incompatible, uncertain, or morally significant.

Spectacle may intensify sensation, but pressure depends on changing choice conditions.

### Current Evidence

Strongly visible in CASE-0003 and CASE-0004.

### Confidence

Moderate.

### Related Hypotheses

H-005, H-009, H-018.

### Engineering Implication

The Story Engine should evaluate the structure and cost of alternatives, not danger magnitude alone.

## NP-005 — Transformative Failure

### Statement

Failure contributes to momentum when it changes knowledge, identity, relationship, resources, pressure, or future possibility rather than merely delaying success.

### Current Evidence

- Neo's failures alter understanding and commitment
- the Green Place failure changes the objective in *Fury Road*
- repeated losses narrow and deepen Theo's obligation in *Children of Men*

### Confidence

Moderate.

### Related Hypotheses

H-005.

### Engineering Implication

Failure outcomes should produce state transitions rather than automatic reset.

## NP-006 — Institutional Inertia and Agency

### Statement

Institutions feel independent when they preserve incentives, memory, resource dependencies, factions, and policies beyond the protagonist's immediate presence.

### Current Evidence

Most strongly supported by CASE-0004; also visible in CASE-0003.

### Confidence

Low to moderate.

### Related Hypotheses

H-012, H-016.

### Engineering Implication

Institutions require autonomous update cycles and cannot exist only as scenes requested by the Story Engine.

## NP-007 — Visible World Operation

### Statement

World understanding strengthens when rules and systems produce observable effects through behavior, environment, resource flow, and consequence.

Explanation is most effective when it participates in causality rather than substituting for it.

### Current Evidence

- Strong: CASE-0003 and CASE-0004
- Mixed negative-control evidence: CASE-0002
- Complementary revelatory evidence: CASE-0001

### Confidence

Moderate.

### Related Hypotheses

H-003, H-017, H-019.

### Engineering Implication

World rules must generate observable outcomes before explanatory narration is considered.

## NP-008 — Momentum as Accumulated State Change

### Statement

Narrative momentum is the accumulation of consequential state change, not the speed or quantity of events.

### Current Evidence

Supported across the first four pilot cases, with CASE-0002 providing contrast between event density and accumulated meaning.

### Confidence

Moderate.

### Related Hypotheses

H-001, H-005, H-009, H-018.

### Engineering Implication

Momentum evaluation must track persistent changes in world, human, relationship, pressure, knowledge, and possibility state.

## NP-009 — Possibility and Hope

### Statement

Hope can increase without danger decreasing when a credible path of continuation, protection, repair, or transformation becomes available.

### Current Evidence

Most strongly supported by CASE-0004; partially supported by CASE-0003.

### Confidence

Low.

### Related Hypotheses

H-020.

### Engineering Implication

Hope should be represented as credible valued possibility, not as positive tone or low threat.

## NP-010 — Selective Closure

### Statement

A resolution may feel earned when it closes the dominant pressure while preserving secondary uncertainty, consequence, and future history.

### Current Evidence

Supported by CASE-0001, CASE-0003, and CASE-0004.

### Confidence

Moderate.

### Related Hypotheses

H-010.

### Engineering Implication

The Story Engine must distinguish dominant pressure from every unresolved thread.

## Governance

1. Narrative Physics remains downstream of Observatory evidence.
2. Contradicting cases must be recorded alongside supporting cases.
3. A law may be narrowed, split, combined, rejected, or retired.
4. Cross-medium claims require cross-medium evidence.
5. Engine requirements derived from a law must preserve its confidence and scope.
6. Exceptions should first be examined as scope conditions, not dismissed as bad storytelling.

## Working Theory

> Narrative Intelligence is the maintenance of interacting causal and interpretive models through time. Its apparent laws concern how state persists, propagates, conflicts, and changes what remains possible.
