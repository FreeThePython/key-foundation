# Narrative Observatory Pilot Corpus

## Purpose

The first Observatory cohort is designed to test the methodology, not to confirm a preferred theory of storytelling.

The corpus deliberately includes works with different combinations of craft reputation, audience response, commercial outcome, and historical reevaluation. All six are feature films in speculative or science-fiction-adjacent genres, which creates enough common ground for comparison while preserving meaningful variation.

## Selection Principles

The pilot corpus should:

- contain both admired and unsuccessful works
- separate narrative craft from market performance
- include at least one work whose reputation changed over time
- include both original and adaptation-based material
- expose the methodology to different world-building burdens
- avoid treating awards, revenue, or aggregate ratings as ground truth
- produce hypotheses that can later be tested across other media and cultures

## Pilot Cohort

### CASE-0001 — The Matrix (1999)

**Status:** Initial pilot analysis complete.  
**Research role:** acclaimed and commercially successful original film with enduring cultural influence.

Primary questions:

- How does hidden truth generate mystery before revelation?
- How does the film connect knowledge, identity, belief, and agency?
- Why does the central revelation expand rather than collapse possibility?
- How does a tightly authored story create the perception of meaningful choice?

### CASE-0002 — The Last Airbender (2010)

**Status:** Initial pilot analysis complete.  
**Research role:** widely rejected adaptation of highly regarded source material.

Primary questions:

- What is lost when relational and developmental material is compressed?
- How does adaptation fidelity differ from narrative effectiveness?
- What happens when exposition replaces experienced causality?
- How do knowledge, desire, relationship, and identity become disconnected?

### CASE-0003 — Mad Max: Fury Road (2015)

**Research role:** highly acclaimed action film with unusually strong visual causality and limited explanatory dialogue.

Primary questions:

- Can world coherence be established primarily through visible consequence?
- How does supporting-character agency strengthen the world?
- How does repetition become escalation rather than redundancy?
- How does the story preserve clarity at sustained intensity?

### CASE-0004 — Children of Men (2006)

**Research role:** critically admired work whose initial commercial performance was comparatively limited.

Primary questions:

- How does world history remain active without extensive exposition?
- How does reluctant agency become moral commitment?
- How do background institutions create narrative pressure?
- What is the relationship between bleakness, hope, and emotional investment?

### CASE-0005 — Jurassic World (2015)

**Research role:** extraordinary commercial success with more divided assessments of narrative craft.

Primary questions:

- Which narrative weaknesses can spectacle, brand recognition, nostalgia, and accessibility offset?
- Where do character decisions serve causality versus franchise necessity?
- How should commercial success be recorded without treating it as proof of narrative quality?
- What does audience enjoyment reveal that critical interpretation may miss?

### CASE-0006 — Blade Runner (1982)

**Research role:** initially polarizing and commercially disappointing work that gained major long-term stature.

Primary questions:

- How should the Observatory model changing reputation?
- Can atmosphere, ambiguity, and thematic density compensate for weak conventional momentum?
- How do alternate cuts change agency, identity, and interpretation?
- What distinguishes productive ambiguity from missing causality?

## Comparison Structure

The pilot will support several controlled comparisons:

- **The Matrix / Blade Runner:** immediate impact versus reputation accumulated over time
- **Children of Men / Jurassic World:** critical admiration versus market reach
- **Mad Max: Fury Road / The Last Airbender:** visual storytelling that preserves causality versus visual compression that weakens it
- **The Matrix / The Last Airbender:** revelation that expands agency versus exposition that reduces participation

## Order of Study

1. The Matrix
2. The Last Airbender
3. Mad Max: Fury Road
4. Children of Men
5. Jurassic World
6. Blade Runner

The second study is intentionally a sharp contrast with the first. If the methodology can only describe why an admired work succeeds, it is not yet useful. It must also identify failure without collapsing into mockery, hindsight, or unsupported certainty.

## Pilot Exit Criteria

The pilot is complete when:

- all six case studies use the same core evidence structure
- disagreements and uncertainties are explicitly recorded
- at least five hypotheses receive meaningful evidence from multiple cases
- at least one initial hypothesis is weakened, revised, or rejected
- the methodology is updated based on observed limitations
- findings are translated into candidate requirements for the World, Human, and Story Engines

## Foundational Principle

> A useful research corpus does not merely contain great stories. It contains stories whose differences can teach us something.
