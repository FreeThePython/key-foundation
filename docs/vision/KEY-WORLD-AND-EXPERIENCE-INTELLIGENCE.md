# KEY World and Experience Intelligence

## North Star

KEY is an intelligence architecture for playable possibility.

It creates, operates, and adapts living worlds by coordinating world state, characters, institutions, narrative, mechanics, gameplay, player experience, and interface through time.

> You do not simply replay a KEY game and receive different content. You enter another realization of the game itself.

## The Full Vision

A KEY world may contain different histories, cosmologies, political systems, cultures, institutions, economies, conflicts, characters, relationships, mysteries, and future possibilities.

But KEY is larger than procedural world generation or adaptive storytelling. It is intended to reason about the form of play itself.

Different realizations may emphasize:

- diplomacy
- warfare
- exploration
- survival
- investigation
- governance
- trade
- social manipulation
- hero-based quests
- construction
- resource management

The world should not merely contain different plots. Its systems, challenge structure, progression, information priorities, and interface may also adapt while preserving coherent identity and usability.

## Six Interacting Intelligences

### World Intelligence

Models cosmology, history, geography, culture, politics, institutions, economies, resources, rules, and active pressures.

### Human Intelligence

Models characters as situated agents with knowledge, beliefs, desires, fears, memories, relationships, identities, and independent agency.

### Narrative Intelligence

Models pressure, possibility, choice, consequence, revelation, transformation, convergence, and resolution.

### Game Intelligence

Models mechanics, rules, challenge, mastery, progression, economies, combat, exploration, social play, and system interaction.

### Experience Intelligence

Models player learning, preference, friction, pacing, difficulty, engagement, emotional rhythm, and perceived possibility.

### Interface Intelligence

Models what information matters now, how it should be represented, which interactions should remain stable, and how the presentation may adapt without sacrificing usability.

## Adaptive Game Design

KEY may eventually alter the composition of play in response to the world and the player.

A player drawn toward court politics may encounter deeper negotiation, reputation, leverage, succession, and institutional systems. Another player may turn the same initial world toward exploration, ancient mysteries, or warfare.

This adaptation must not be arbitrary. It must remain:

- causally grounded
- legible to the player
- consistent with the world's rules
- compatible with a stable interaction grammar
- reversible or explainable when possible
- bounded by the identity of the experience

## Stable Identity, Variable Expression

A KEY experience may vary in content, structure, mechanics, UI emphasis, pacing, and role. It must still preserve a recognizable design language.

Adaptive interface is not arbitrary interface mutation.

Adaptive gameplay is not random feature switching.

Generative systems must preserve coherence, learnability, fairness, and authored intent at the level of principles.

## KEY as a World Operating System

KEY is not one game and not one genre.

It is a world operating system on which many forms of interactive experience may run.

Games are the first application. The same architecture may later support simulations, persistent shared worlds, tabletop systems, educational environments, narrative media, and original intellectual property.

## The Prototype Is Not the Product

The first prototype is deliberately tiny.

> The prototype is not a miniature version of the final product. It is a laboratory for testing the smallest useful behaviors that KEY must eventually perform.

The prototype must not attempt to prove the entire North Star. It should test one bounded claim at a time and expand only when the current layer demonstrates coherent, observable, and repeatable behavior.

The long-term vision protects direction. The prototype protects learning.

## Development Doctrine

KEY will grow through staged proof:

```text
One interaction
    ↓
One relationship network
    ↓
One local conflict
    ↓
One settlement
    ↓
One institution
    ↓
One region
    ↓
One world
    ↓
Adaptive forms of play
```

Each stage must earn the next.

## Long-Term Promise

KEY does not promise infinite content.

It promises meaningful possibility.

It does not merely generate stories inside games.

It seeks to create games whose worlds, stories, systems, and forms of play can develop differently while remaining coherent, consequential, and worth caring about.
