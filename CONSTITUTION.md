# The Constitution of KEY

## The Foundational Principles of Narrative Intelligence

**KEY — We Unlock Worlds**

---

## Preamble

Stories are among humanity's oldest technologies.

Long before writing, governments, or machines, stories carried memory, taught wisdom, preserved identity, and helped people understand themselves.

For centuries, stories have been constrained by the limits of their medium. Books tell one version. Films show one perspective. Games often reduce choice to predetermined branches.

KEY exists because a new medium is possible.

A medium where worlds possess their own truth.

Where people possess their own agency.

Where history continues beyond observation.

Where choices reshape possibility.

Where mystery precedes discovery.

Where consequence becomes memory.

Where intelligence serves coherence rather than convenience.

This Constitution governs every philosophy, architecture, engine, world, story, and future expression of KEY. It is intended to outlive any particular technology.

Models will change.

Engines will evolve.

Programming languages will disappear.

The Constitution remains.

---

## Article I — Story Is the Product

KEY does not exist merely to generate dialogue, quests, scenes, or content.

KEY exists to create worlds capable of producing meaningful stories.

Every system shall ultimately serve story.

---

## Article II — Truth Exists Before Observation

Objective truth exists independently of the player, characters, cultures, institutions, and narration.

Discovery reveals truth. It does not create it.

Truth may be hidden, disputed, forgotten, or misunderstood. It may not be arbitrary.

---

## Article III — Worlds Live Beyond Their Audience

The world exists independently of the observer.

Time advances. Children grow. Kings age. Wars continue. Harvests succeed or fail. Religions evolve. Institutions rise and fall.

The player joins history. The player does not suspend it.

---

## Article IV — Choice Creates Consequence

Every meaningful choice alters possibility.

Action matters.

Inaction matters.

Delay matters.

Silence matters.

Misunderstanding matters.

Sacrifice matters.

No meaningful choice disappears without leaving consequence.

---

## Article V — Consequence Creates Memory

Memory is not merely a log of events.

Memory is the accumulation of consequences.

The world remembers through changed relationships, institutions, beliefs, landscapes, opportunities, dangers, and histories.

---

## Article VI — Meaning Emerges Through Interpretation

Truth remains fixed while understanding evolves.

Great stories do not rewrite the past for convenience. They illuminate it through discovery, revelation, and reinterpretation.

---

## Article VII — Mystery Begins With Hidden Truth

Mystery shall not originate from randomness disguised as depth.

Every legitimate mystery begins with a reality that exists before the question is asked.

Questions exist because answers already exist, even when those answers remain inaccessible, incomplete, or permanently unknowable.

---

## Article VIII — Every Person Possesses Agency

Every person possesses desires, fears, goals, relationships, beliefs, memories, limits, and blind spots.

No one exists merely to advance another person's story.

Agency belongs to every life, not only to protagonists.

---

## Article IX — Intelligence Serves Coherence

Artificial intelligence exists to deepen consistency, causality, responsiveness, and meaning.

It may propose. It may imagine. It may surprise.

It may not violate world truth, character agency, knowledge boundaries, or established consequence for convenience.

---

## Article X — Canon Must Be Earned

Generated material is proposal, not truth.

Creation proposes.

Review challenges.

Revision strengthens.

Canon requires scrutiny.

Nothing becomes part of a world merely because a model produced it.

---

## Article XI — Systems Exist to Deepen Story

Mechanics are servants, never masters.

Combat, economics, politics, crafting, magic, relationships, diplomacy, exploration, and every future system belong in KEY only insofar as they deepen meaningful narrative.

A system that cannot affect meaning, choice, consequence, character, or world state is ornamental.

---

## Article XII — Discovery Is Superior to Exposition

Wonder precedes explanation.

Curiosity precedes instruction.

The player should learn because they explored, questioned, listened, risked, remembered, and connected what they found.

The world should not interrupt itself merely to explain itself.

---

## Article XIII — Worlds Must Remain Capable of Surprise

No world shall become completely solved.

Every world should retain mystery, uncertainty, undiscovered relationships, and unrealized possibility.

Surprise must emerge from truth and causality, not contradiction.

---

## Article XIV — Simplicity Reveals Depth

Complexity should emerge from interaction rather than unnecessary rules.

Every individual system should remain understandable.

Together, those systems should become inexhaustible.

---

## Article XV — KEY Unlocks Worlds

KEY is not defined by a single game, engine, model, or medium.

KEY is a philosophy and system for creating living realities capable of producing meaningful experiences across games, novels, films, education, simulation, and forms of storytelling yet to be imagined.

---

## Article XVI — The Constitution Precedes the Engine

No implementation may supersede these principles.

Architecture shall derive from philosophy.

Software shall derive from architecture.

Technology shall remain the servant of purpose.

When uncertainty arises, this Constitution takes precedence over convenience, novelty, spectacle, and efficiency.

---

## Constitutional Order

The foundational hierarchy of KEY is:

1. **The Constitution** — what KEY must always believe.
2. **The Manifesto** — why those principles matter and how they shape narrative.
3. **The Lexicon** — the canonical language used to express those principles.
4. **The Architecture** — how the principles become systems.
5. **The Prototype** — the first bounded proof that those systems can work.
6. **The Code** — a replaceable implementation of an enduring purpose.

A downstream document may clarify an upstream principle. It may not silently contradict it.

---

## Amendment Process

This Constitution is intentionally stable but not infallible.

An amendment must:

1. identify the existing article affected;
2. explain the problem that cannot be solved through clarification alone;
3. describe the effect on the Manifesto, Lexicon, Architecture, Prototype, and evaluation standards;
4. receive independent continuity and philosophical review;
5. update every downstream contradiction created by the amendment;
6. be recorded explicitly as an amendment rather than hidden inside unrelated edits.

Constitutional change should be rare because every downstream system depends upon it.

---

## Closing Declaration

A world is not alive because it contains people.

A world is alive because it possesses truth.

Because it remembers.

Because it changes.

Because it surprises.

Because it continues after we leave it.

The purpose of KEY is not merely to generate stories.

The purpose of KEY is to unlock worlds where meaningful stories become inevitable.

> **We do not write stories. We create worlds that remember, change, and surprise.**
