# KEY Narrative Hypothesis Register

## Purpose

The Hypothesis Register prevents KEY from turning intuition into doctrine.

Every research claim must remain traceable to evidence, counterevidence, context, and uncertainty. A hypothesis may be supported, weakened, revised, or retired without threatening the identity of the project.

## Status Definitions

- **Proposed** — clearly stated but not yet studied systematically
- **Under Study** — active evidence collection has begun
- **Provisionally Supported** — multiple observations support the claim, but broader validation is required
- **Mixed** — evidence varies by context, medium, genre, culture, or interpretation
- **Provisionally Rejected** — available evidence weighs against the present formulation
- **Revised** — replaced by a narrower, clearer, or more accurate formulation
- **Retired** — no longer useful, testable, or compatible with the framework

No status means “proven universally.”

## Confidence Definitions

- **Low** — limited evidence or major unresolved alternatives
- **Moderate** — converging evidence with meaningful uncertainty remaining
- **High** — repeated, direct, cross-case evidence with few credible alternatives

## Governance Rules

1. Every hypothesis receives a stable ID.
2. Supporting and contradicting evidence must be recorded together.
3. A hypothesis cannot be promoted solely because it sounds compatible with the Manifesto.
4. Cross-medium claims require cross-medium evidence.
5. Cross-cultural claims require culturally diverse evidence.
6. A score or model output is not independent evidence unless validated against human observation.
7. Failed hypotheses remain in the register.
8. Revised hypotheses retain links to earlier formulations.
9. System requirements derived from research must cite the hypothesis and evidence that motivated them.
10. The absence of contradiction is not confirmation.

## Foundational Principle

> **KEY becomes more credible each time it records where it may be wrong.**
