# KEY World Engine Specification

## Purpose

The World Engine maintains canonical world state and advances systems whose behavior does not depend on protagonist observation.

## Research Principle

> A world feels alive when its history, institutions, resources, rules, and conflicts continue to generate consequences beyond the immediate scene.

## Core Responsibilities

- maintain canonical truth
- represent locations, resources, artifacts, rules, and constraints
- preserve active history
- update institutions and collective systems
- propagate physical, social, economic, and political consequences
- expose observable effects without requiring explanatory narration

## Requirements

### W-001 — Independent World Progression

World systems must be able to advance without direct protagonist participation.

**Evidence:** CASE-0001, CASE-0003, CASE-0004  
**Related hypotheses:** H-012, H-016  
**Confidence:** Moderate

### W-002 — Active History

Past events must remain connected to current rules, relationships, resources, institutions, geography, or public belief when causally relevant.

**Evidence:** CASE-0003, CASE-0004  
**Related hypotheses:** H-001, H-016  
**Confidence:** Moderate

### W-003 — Institutional State

Institutions must preserve incentives, roles, factions, resources, policies, reputation, and memory beyond individual members.

**Evidence:** CASE-0004; partial support from CASE-0003  
**Related hypotheses:** H-016  
**Confidence:** Low to moderate

### W-004 — Visible Operation

World rules should produce observable effects through behavior, environment, resource flow, and consequence.

**Evidence:** CASE-0002, CASE-0003, CASE-0004  
**Related hypotheses:** H-017, H-019  
**Confidence:** Moderate

### W-005 — Resource Causality

Scarce resources must constrain action, affect power, and change possibility when the world establishes them as important.

**Evidence:** CASE-0003, CASE-0004  
**Confidence:** Moderate

### W-006 — Propagation Paths

Consequences must travel through explicit physical, informational, relational, institutional, or cultural connections.

**Evidence:** Cross-case methodological requirement  
**Related hypotheses:** H-001, H-012  
**Confidence:** Low

### W-007 — Canonical and Contested State

The engine must distinguish objective world truth from public belief, institutional claims, propaganda, myth, and unresolved interpretation.

**Evidence:** CASE-0001, CASE-0004  
**Related hypotheses:** H-003, H-007, H-013  
**Confidence:** Moderate

## Non-Goals

The World Engine does not choose the most dramatic next event, write prose, or force entities into predetermined plot roles.

## Validation Questions

- Does the world change when the focal agent is absent?
- Can current conditions be traced to prior state?
- Do institutions pursue recognizable incentives?
- Do resources and rules alter behavior?
- Can observers disagree without corrupting canonical truth?

## Open Questions

- What update frequency is sufficient for off-screen systems?
- Which emergent systems require dedicated models?
- How much simulation can be abstracted without losing causal credibility?
