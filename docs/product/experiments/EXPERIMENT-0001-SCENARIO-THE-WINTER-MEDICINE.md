# EXPERIMENT-0001 Scenario — The Winter Medicine

## Purpose

This scenario is the first authored test fixture for EXPERIMENT-0001. It is intentionally small, but it must still be believable enough for player judgment to matter.

The scenario does not exist to prove that KEY can generate dramatic prose. It exists to test whether knowledge, motives, relationships, memory, pressure, and consequence can interact coherently through play.

## Player-Facing Premise

A severe winter storm has isolated the village of Greyhaven. The healer possesses one remaining dose of fever medicine. Two people may die without it.

- Lysa Venn, the mill owner's twelve-year-old daughter, is weakening from winter fever.
- Elian Vale, a wounded courier, may die from infection after crossing the mountain pass to warn Greyhaven of an approaching raiding party.

The village reeve died three days earlier. The player is a respected outsider temporarily asked to mediate until the village council can assemble.

The medicine will almost certainly save only one patient.

## Why the Decision Matters

The medicine is linked to more than survival.

- Tomas Venn controls most of the village's remaining grain.
- Mara Holt, the healer, has sworn never to let wealth determine treatment.
- Elian possesses incomplete but urgent information about the raiders.
- Elian once saved Mara's younger brother.
- Tomas previously concealed grain during an earlier shortage.
- The storm prevents outside assistance.

No outcome should be treated as morally or strategically obvious.

## Canonical Truth

These facts are objectively true at the start of the experiment.

1. There is exactly one effective dose of medicine.
2. Lysa will probably survive if treated within thirty minutes.
3. Elian will probably survive if treated within forty-five minutes.
4. Without medicine, either patient may still survive, but the probability is low.
5. Elian saw at least twenty raiders moving toward Greyhaven.
6. Elian does not know whether they intend to attack, extort, or pass through.
7. The eastern watchtower is currently unmanned.
8. Tomas controls approximately seventy percent of the accessible grain.
9. Tomas concealed grain during the previous winter shortage.
10. Mara knows Tomas concealed grain but cannot prove how much.
11. Mara promised her dying mentor that treatment would never be sold to the highest bidder.
12. Elian saved Mara's brother Corin from drowning six years earlier.
13. Tomas paid for half of the medicine shipment, but he did not purchase ownership of an individual dose.
14. The village has enough grain for eight days if Tomas releases his stores.
15. The storm will block the northern road for at least two days.
16. Delay changes both patients' survival probabilities.

## Public Starting Knowledge

The player initially knows:

- one dose remains
- two patients need it
- Tomas's daughter is one of them
- Elian arrived wounded with a warning
- Tomas controls substantial grain
- Mara is the healer and current custodian of the medicine

The player does not initially know:

- the exact survival windows
- Tomas's previous concealment
- Mara's promise to her mentor
- Elian's past rescue of Corin
- the uncertainty in Elian's warning
- the exact condition of the eastern watchtower

## Character Models

### Mara Holt — Village Healer

**Public role:** healer and current custodian of the medicine

**Core values:** medical duty, fairness, professional independence, protection of the vulnerable

**Primary desire:** allocate the medicine according to medical and communal need

**Primary fear:** becoming complicit in a decision where wealth buys survival

**Knowledge:**

- both patients' current condition
- approximate treatment windows
- Tomas's past concealment
- her promise to her mentor
- Elian's rescue of Corin
- Tomas funded part of the medicine shipment

**False or uncertain beliefs:**

- believes Tomas will likely withhold grain if denied, but does not know whether he truly will
- suspects Elian's warning may be incomplete

**Relationships:**

- distrusts Tomas
- feels unresolved gratitude toward Elian
- feels protective responsibility toward Lysa as a child and patient

**Moral boundary:** will not openly sell treatment for grain

**Possible pressure response:** may retain custody and refuse the player's order if she believes the decision is corrupt or medically indefensible

### Tomas Venn — Mill Owner and Father

**Public role:** mill owner, grain holder, father of Lysa

**Core values:** family duty, control, reciprocity, status, practical survival

**Primary desire:** secure the medicine for Lysa

**Primary fear:** watching his daughter die while others claim moral authority over his resources

**Knowledge:**

- Lysa is deteriorating
- he financed half the medicine shipment
- he controls most accessible grain
- he concealed grain during the prior shortage
- Elian claims raiders are approaching

**False or uncertain beliefs:**

- believes his financial contribution gives him the strongest legitimate claim
- believes Mara's preference for Elian is partly personal
- underestimates how much the village resents his prior behavior

**Relationships:**

- distrusts Mara's impartiality
- has no personal loyalty to Elian
- sees the player as potentially useful, dangerous, or illegitimate depending on conduct

**Moral boundary:** will not intentionally harm Lysa, but may endanger the village by withholding grain

**Possible pressure response:** may offer grain conditionally, threaten withdrawal, attempt to take the medicine, or later retaliate socially or economically

### Elian Vale — Courier

**Public role:** wounded courier carrying a warning

**Core values:** duty, courage, restraint, communal survival

**Primary desire:** ensure the village acts on the warning

**Primary fear:** dying after failing to communicate enough to protect Greyhaven

**Knowledge:**

- observed approximately twenty raiders
- knows their direction and rough speed
- knows the eastern watchtower appeared unmanned
- does not know the raiders' exact intent
- remembers saving Corin

**False or uncertain beliefs:**

- believes the village may have less time than it actually does
- believes Mara's gratitude may distort her judgment

**Relationships:**

- respects Mara but does not want her to choose him from obligation
- distrusts Tomas's use of leverage
- has no established relationship with the player

**Moral boundary:** will not knowingly exaggerate the certainty of an attack to obtain medicine

**Possible pressure response:** may refuse the medicine, reveal additional information, request that the player prioritize the village, or privately ask Mara not to act from debt

## Initial State

- Time: minute 0
- Medicine location: locked cabinet in Mara's clinic
- Medicine control: Mara possesses the key
- Lysa location: adjacent treatment room
- Elian location: clinic meeting room
- Tomas location: clinic meeting room
- Mara location: clinic meeting room
- Player location: clinic meeting room
- Grain control: Tomas
- Eastern watchtower status: unmanned
- Village council availability: earliest assembly in sixty minutes

## Pressure Timeline

### Minute 10

Lysa's fever worsens visibly.

### Minute 20

A villager reports that Tomas's grain store remains locked.

### Minute 30

Lysa's expected benefit from medicine begins to decline sharply.

### Minute 35

Elian becomes less coherent and may lose the ability to communicate details.

### Minute 45

Elian's expected benefit from medicine begins to decline sharply.

### Minute 60

The council can assemble, but either patient's position may already be irreversible.

The engine must not force every event to occur identically if earlier actions alter conditions.

## Meaningful Player Approaches

The scenario supports, but does not prescribe:

- medical triage
- bargaining with Tomas
- rejecting coercive bargaining
- asking Elian to share all intelligence first
- placing the medicine under neutral custody
- making conditional promises
- seeking public witnesses
- delaying for the council
- attempting to split or substitute the dose, even though this may fail
- allowing Mara to decide
- transferring responsibility to Tomas
- refusing the role

## Required Divergence

Different playthroughs must be capable of changing:

- who receives medicine
- whether Tomas releases grain
- Mara's trust in the player
- Tomas's trust or hostility
- whether Elian communicates the full warning
- whether the village mans the eastern watchtower
- which promises or resentments persist
- which future actions remain available

## Scenario Failure Conditions

The scenario itself has failed if:

- one choice is obviously correct to nearly every tester
- characters exist only as arguments for positions
- hidden facts function as arbitrary twists
- consequences cannot reasonably follow from the starting state
- the player lacks enough agency to pursue an unexpected but sensible approach
- the emotional stakes overwhelm the ability to inspect causal behavior

## Authoring Constraint

The scenario is controlled fiction. It must remain stable across baseline tests. KEY may operate character decisions and consequences, but it may not silently rewrite canonical starting facts to improve drama.
