# Narrative Observatory Terms

## Narrative Observatory

A research system that studies existing narratives through the KEY framework to identify structural patterns, causal relationships, tradeoffs, failures, and enduring strengths that can improve Narrative Intelligence.

The Narrative Observatory does not exist to rank stories, imitate successful works, or optimize for popularity. It exists to test KEY's assumptions against evidence.

## Narrative Corpus

A curated collection of narrative works, metadata, analyses, comparisons, and research observations used by the Narrative Observatory.

A Narrative Corpus may contain works from multiple media, genres, cultures, and eras, but every inclusion should have a stated research purpose.

## Narrative Case Study

A structured analysis of one narrative work using a common observational framework while preserving the work's medium, genre, cultural, historical, and production context.

## Narrative Hypothesis

A testable claim about a relationship between narrative structures, character dynamics, causal patterns, audience outcomes, or contextual conditions.

A Narrative Hypothesis must identify evidence that could weaken or falsify it.

## Comparative Narrative Set

A deliberately selected group of narratives examined together to isolate meaningful similarities, differences, patterns, or tradeoffs.

## Structural Observation

A documented claim about how a narrative is constructed or how its components interact, separated as clearly as possible from personal preference or evaluative judgment.

## Reception Signal

An observed outcome associated with how a narrative was received, including audience response, critical response, longevity, rewatchability, awards, commercial performance, or cultural influence.

A Reception Signal is evidence to interpret, not a direct measure of narrative quality.

## Narrative Tradeoff

A condition in which strengthening one narrative quality may weaken, constrain, or complicate another.

Examples may include freedom versus pacing, mystery versus clarity, realism versus compression, or supporting-character autonomy versus protagonist focus.

## Script Dissection

A scene-level and causal analysis of a screenplay that traces objectives, information, pressure, reversals, consequences, relationship changes, setup, revelation, and payoff.

## Research Finding

A supported conclusion produced through Narrative Observatory analysis.

A Research Finding does not become a canonical KEY principle until it survives review, remains compatible with the Constitution, and can be translated into a clear principle, definition, evaluation criterion, or system requirement.

## Narrative Evidence

Documented material used to support or challenge a Narrative Hypothesis, including structural observations, comparative analysis, script changes, production differences, reception signals, and contextual information.

## Interpretive Uncertainty

The explicitly recorded limits, ambiguities, disagreements, missing information, or context dependence affecting a narrative analysis.

KEY must preserve Interpretive Uncertainty rather than presenting contested judgment as objective truth.
