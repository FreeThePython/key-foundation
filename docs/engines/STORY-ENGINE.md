# KEY Story Engine Specification

## Purpose

The Story Engine identifies and shapes meaningful pressure, possibility, consequence, revelation, transformation, and resolution without replacing world and human causality with predetermined plot.

## Research Principle

> Narrative momentum is accumulated consequential state change, not the speed or quantity of events.

## Core Responsibilities

- detect active pressures and unresolved commitments
- track available and perceived possibilities
- identify consequential decision points
- preserve agency while supporting coherence
- evaluate escalation through changing alternatives
- recognize revelation, reinterpretation, failure, and transformation
- distinguish dominant pressure from secondary unresolved threads

## Requirements

### S-001 — State-Based Momentum

Momentum must be evaluated through persistent change in world, human, relationship, knowledge, pressure, and possibility state.

**Evidence:** CASE-0001 through CASE-0004  
**Related hypotheses:** H-001, H-005, H-009, H-018  
**Confidence:** Moderate

### S-002 — Pressure Through Meaningful Constraint

Escalation should increase the cost, incompatibility, uncertainty, or moral weight of available alternatives rather than merely increase spectacle.

**Evidence:** CASE-0003, CASE-0004; contrast from CASE-0002  
**Related hypotheses:** H-009, H-018  
**Confidence:** Moderate

### S-003 — Transformative Failure

Failure should be capable of changing knowledge, identity, relationship, resources, objective, or future possibility.

**Evidence:** CASE-0001, CASE-0003, CASE-0004  
**Related hypotheses:** H-005  
**Confidence:** Moderate

### S-004 — Earned Revelation

Revelations should connect to prior observable effects, evidence, contradiction, or hidden causal structure.

**Evidence:** CASE-0001; contrast from CASE-0002  
**Related hypotheses:** H-003, H-013  
**Confidence:** Moderate

### S-005 — Agency Preservation

The engine must not remove meaningful alternatives solely to force a preferred plot outcome. Convergence may preserve agency when prior choices continue to alter relationship, identity, knowledge, cost, or consequence.

**Evidence:** Pilot synthesis; authored-film evidence remains indirect  
**Related hypotheses:** H-007, H-011  
**Confidence:** Low

### S-006 — Selective Closure

Resolution must close or transform the dominant pressure without requiring every active thread to end.

**Evidence:** CASE-0001, CASE-0003, CASE-0004  
**Related hypotheses:** H-010  
**Confidence:** Moderate

### S-007 — Hope as Credible Possibility

The engine may increase hope by establishing a credible valued continuation even when danger remains high.

**Evidence:** CASE-0004; partial support from CASE-0003  
**Related hypotheses:** H-020  
**Confidence:** Low

### S-008 — Narrative Load Distribution

The engine should recognize that explanation can be carried by dialogue, environment, behavior, action, relationship, and consequence.

**Evidence:** CASE-0001 through CASE-0004  
**Related hypotheses:** H-017, H-019  
**Confidence:** Moderate

## Non-Goals

The Story Engine does not own canonical truth, manufacture arbitrary character motivation, or erase consequence to restore a preferred outline.

## Validation Questions

- What state changed and why does it matter later?
- Which alternatives became unavailable or more costly?
- Did failure transform the possibility graph?
- Was the revelation causally prepared?
- Did resolution address the dominant pressure?

## Open Questions

- How should dramatic opportunity be ranked without enforcing formula?
- When should the engine intervene, suggest, or remain silent?
- How can interactive convergence be evaluated across multiple playthroughs?
